Keto Diet

A keto diet is well known for being a low carb diet, where the body produces ketones in the liver to be used as energy.


Keep in mind that it's a high fat diet so nutrient intake should be 70% fats, 25% protein and 5% carbohydrate. The lower the carb the better!

What is Ketosis

Ketosis is a natural process the body initiates to help us survive when food intake is low. During this state, we produce ketones, which are produced from the breakdown of fats in the liver.

What to eat

  • Fats- coconut oils, Mct, cheese, high fat cream, butter, ghee etc
  • Meats- fish, beef, lamb, poultry, eggs etc.
  • Leafy Greens- spinach, kale, etc.
  • Ground veges- broccoli, cauliflower etc
  • Nuts- walnut, sunflower seeds etc
  • Fruits- avocado, resberries, blueberries, blackberries etc
  • Sweetners- Stevia, erythritol etc

Not to Eat

  • Grains- whole wheat, rice, corn, cereal, quinoa, all purpose flour, oats
  • Sugar- hhoney, agave, granulated processed sugar
  • Fruits & veges- banana, apple, carrots, potato, yams etc

Keto Flu

A common side effect

When transitioning to keto, you may feel some slight discomfort including fatigue, headache, nausea, cramps, etc but it goes away in few days
